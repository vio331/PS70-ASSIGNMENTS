Idea_1 : Playful pavillion

I'm concurrently taking a class where i get to design a pedestrian bridge, pavillion or comparable small scale landscape structure. My thought would be to link the two classes and learn to create a component of the design that reacts to an external element. This can be wind, heat, motion or sound - still not 100% sure. It could be a plyground pavillion with parts that open up if kids are yelling near by; to maybe just learning to make a simple dynamic sun shader. This is the more ambitious idea and will depend on what type or kind of sensors I can build.



Idea_2 : Anti Cat Device

My friend has a cat - Olive - that likes to knock items off her center table to wake her up when he is hungry in the morning. He starts almost consistently at 6:30 beginning with the alarm clock that makes a lot of noise. Maybe this is a device that dogdes Olive's paw. It could move in a different direction and release a small treat in the opposite direction that it goes to get him away from it. Could be programmed to release more treats at the begining and less closer to when she wakes up at 7:00. Alternatively it could spray water at him, but that's too mean...or when its about to get knocked over. Could also double as a play toy



Idea_3 : Pixel Drawing

A drawing machine that turns a vector or raster image into something on paper - basicaly a quasi printer. I would like to start this by figuring out how to make it with a pen or paper. But I love pixel art as a form of representation. And think it would be fun to make the pen a pixel stamp and not a line? Greyscale images for now but maybe I the stamp could be adjustable. A smaller stamp would maybe create a more recognizable image, and a larger one would be more abstract.
